I'm a recent game design and programming graduate looking for jo​bs as a gameplay programmer or technical QA. I'm also open to work on projects on a freelance basis, please feel free to contact me if you'd like to collaborate!

I have experience working with:

  • Unity (C#)
  • Unreal (C++ and Unreal)
  • C# (in and out of engine)
  • C++ (in and out of engine) 
  • Visual studio 
  • Version Control in Github (in groups of 10-20)
  • Jira for project management 

I am a quick learning and if a project requires me to learn a new skillset, engine, or programming language I am fully willing to put the hours into learning it, and have done so before.
I also have basic experience with: Python, SFML, OpenGL, SDL and Gamemaker studio. 
 
I like solving complex problems, while I enjoy playing games my favourite thing is making games or understanding how other games work, and that can often be seen via how you can break them. One of my favourite parts of the development process is debugging and finding issues with projects, getting to the route of them and solving the problem at its core rather than simply patching it. I like to get to get to the best solution, and those solutions are most often found in teams where we can bounce ideas off of each other.

Games offer a variety of different problems to be solved, with entirely different sets of things to solve between projects and even within the same project. Games mix art and technology and immerse people into stories in ways that traditional mediums simply can't do, allowing you to fully immerse a player into a completely new world and give them the ability to interact and make decisions about the direction that world is going in. It's a field that combines all of the things I love to work on and I want to make every project I get my hands on the best it can be, even if that means going out of my comfort zone.
